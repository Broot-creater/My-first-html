Ratio and propotion

  1. A ratio is formed when two quantities are compared by division that is, a/b or a:b.
  2. A ratio is written in its simplest form and has no unit.
  3. If a quantity, increases (or decreases) in the ratio a:b, then the new quantity is b/a of the original quantity, b/a is called the multiplying factor.
  4. If we have to divide a given number A in the ratio a:b:c ,then,
    First part = a / a+b+c x A
    Second part = b/a+b+c x A
    Third part = c/a+b+c x A
  5. An equality of two ratios is called a proportion.The first and fourth terms are called extremes and second and third terms are called means.
  6. Four quantities a,b,c and d are said to be in proportion if a/b =c/d.
  7. In a proportion a:b :: c:d,d is said to be fourth proportion.In a proportion a:b :: c:d,d is said to be fourth proportion.
  8. Three quantities a,b,c are said to be in continued proportion if b2=ac
  9. In the continued proportion a:b :: b:c,b is called the mean proportional between a and c,and c is called the third proportional.
